What is Thila Homam and why is it performed in Rameswaram?+

Thila Homam is a Vedic fire ritual where sesame seeds are offered to appease ancestors, remove Pitru Dosha, and bring liberation (moksha) to departed souls. Rameswaram is the ideal location because its holy Sethu waters and proximity to the Ramanathaswamy Jyotirlinga greatly amplify the ritual's spiritual potency, ensuring departed souls attain liberation.

What Pitru Dosha symptoms does Thila Homam relieve?+

Thila Homam is believed to help relieve common Pitru Dosha symptoms including: unexplained delays in marriage, career or job settlement issues, disunity or conflict in the family, delay in childbirth, recurring financial struggles despite hard work, and health issues without a clear medical cause.

When are the most auspicious days for Thila Homam?+

The most auspicious days for Thila Homam are all Amavasai (new moon days), all Saturdays, Ashtami (8th lunar day), and Shani Dhishai (Saturn transit days). Among these, Amavasai is considered the most powerful day for performing pitru rituals.

How long does a Thila Homam ceremony take?+

A Thila Homam ceremony typically takes 2 to 4 hours depending on the complexity and number of offerings. Manoj Shastrigal explains every step and its meaning throughout the ceremony so participants fully understand the ritual they are witnessing and its spiritual significance.

Can I perform Thila Homam online via E-Puja?+

Yes. The E-Puja service allows you to have Thila Homam performed in Rameswaram on your behalf by Manoj Shastrigal. You join live via video call from anywhere in India and receive the full spiritual blessings of the ceremony without needing to travel.

What is the difference between Thila Homam and Thithi?+

Thila Homam is a Vedic fire ritual specifically to remove Pitru Dosha and liberate departed ancestors. Thithi is a separate ceremony where food offerings (pindams) are made to honour ancestors on their death anniversary. Both serve ancestral reverence but have distinct purposes, methods, and occasions.
